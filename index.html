I am a Ph.D. student in the department of Computer Science and Engineering at The Ohio State University. My research area is natural language processing. My recent work is about large-scale sentence level paraphrase collection in Twitter (EMNLP 2017) and deep neural networks for paraphrase identification (NAACL 2018, COLING 2018). My PhD advisor is professor Wei Xu. Beyond NLP, I attempt to apply AI techniques into other fields and design novel deep neural models to solve real-life problems, e.g. travel time prediction (IJCAI 2019).

I also did research on wireless networks and data center networks. In 2014, I interned at Microsoft Research Asia and solved incast problem in data center by developing rate shaping tool (ICNP 2014). Prior to joining OSU, I received my B.E. in computer science from University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2014, where I did research on facial expression recognition with deep boltzmann machine (ACII 2013).
